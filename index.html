Batch Turkey & black bean chilli
Scales seamlessly — flavor deepens over time
To make 4 days' worth (8 portions total):
1 kg turkey mince · 4 tins black beans · 2 tins chopped tomatoes · 600 ml passata · 4 peppers · 2 onions · 8 garlic cloves · Double all spices · 400 ml GF chicken stock
Cook in the largest pot you own. Use the single-batch method but double all quantities. The chilli takes the same amount of active time regardless of scale — just needs an extra 10 min simmering. Cool completely before portioning into 8 airtight containers (or 4 large ones for 2-person servings). Cook rice separately each day for best texture — it only takes 12 min.
Freezes perfectly up to 3 months. Freeze in individual portions. Defrost overnight in fridge. Fridge-life: 4 days.
Batch Frittata cups
The ultimate high-protein breakfast hack
To make 4 days' worth (24 cups total):
24 large eggs · 300 g spinach · 2 red peppers · 200 g turkey mince · 1 red onion · 60 g goat's cheese (for Mel's 12 cups) · 2 muffin tins or bake in 2 batches
Use 2 muffin tins simultaneously in the oven for efficiency. Clearly label Mel's cups (the ones with goat's cheese) with a toothpick or colour-coded foil. Bake both trays at 180°C for 17–18 min. Allow to cool fully on a rack before stacking in containers — moisture is the enemy.
Fridge: 4 days in airtight containers. Freeze: up to 1 month. Reheat from frozen: 2 min in microwave or 8 min in oven at 170°C. Do not reheat more than once.
Batch Lemon herb chicken thighs
Roast once, map across meals seamlessly
To make 4 days' worth (8 thighs):
8–10 chicken thighs · 3 courgettes · 4 peppers · 400 g cherry tomatoes · 2 red onions · 3 lemons · 8 garlic cloves · Generous olive oil · Quinoa cooked per-day
Marinate overnight (Sunday morning, cook Sunday evening). Use two large roasting trays. Vegetables can be batch-roasted too but store separately from the chicken so they don't go soggy. Keep chicken portions (Dave: 2 thighs, Mel: 1 thigh) in separate containers. Reheat chicken in oven 10 min at 180°C or briefly in microwave. Quinoa is fast enough (15 min) to cook daily.
Fridge: 3–4 days. Freeze: 2 months (freeze without quinoa). Roasted veg fridge: 3 days max — batch only if you'll use within that window.
Batch Thai green curry
Freeze half structural macros, finish clean
To make 4 days' worth (8 portions):
1.2 kg chicken breast · 4 tins coconut milk · 4–5 tbsp GF Thai green curry paste · 2 courgettes · 200 g mangetout · 200 g baby corn · 2 onions · Double garlic and ginger
Cook in a large stockpot or casserole dish. Make 2 separate pots if your largest pan can't hold double batch comfortably — curry quality drops if it's too crowded. Store rice separately. The curry reheats beautifully in a saucepan with a splash of water/coconut milk if it thickens in the fridge.
Fridge: 3 days. Freezes extremely well — up to 3 months. Freeze in meal-sized zip-lock bags laid flat. Reheat from frozen on hob over low heat, stirring, adding a splash of coconut milk.
