Scrambled eggs with turkey bacon & avocado
15 minEgg-heavyGF/DF
Base ingredients
Eggs
Turkey bacon rashers
Avocado
Cherry tomatoes
Olive oil
Salt, pepper, chives
Optional add-ons
Sourdough GF toast (Dave)
Goat's cheese crumble (Mel)
Dave — 2,550 kcal goal
4 large eggs
3 turkey bacon rashers
½ avocado
80 g cherry tomatoes
1 slice GF sourdough
1 tsp olive oil
~520 kcal · 42g P · 28g C · 24g F
Mel — 1,600 kcal goal
3 large eggs
2 turkey bacon rashers
½ avocado
60 g cherry tomatoes
15 g goat's cheese
½ tsp olive oil
~380 kcal · 32g P · 6g C · 26g F
Method
  1. Heat a non-stick pan over medium-high. Cook turkey bacon 2–3 min per side until crispy. Set aside.
  2. Reduce heat to medium-low. Add oil, crack in eggs, season well. Fold gently with a spatula every 30 seconds — remove from heat while slightly underdone as residual heat finishes them.
  3. Halve and slice avocado, season with salt and lemon if desired.
  4. Dave: toast GF sourdough and plate alongside. Mel: crumble goat's cheese over eggs while still warm.
  5. Serve with cherry tomatoes and chives scattered over the top.
Baked egg & vegetable frittata cups
Batch-friendlyEgg-heavyGF/DF
Base (makes 12 cups)
12 large eggs
150 g spinach
1 red pepper, diced
100 g turkey mince or diced turkey
½ red onion, finely diced
Salt, pepper, paprika, dried oregano
Extras
30 g goat's cheese (Mel's cups)
Olive oil spray
Dave
4 frittata cups
+ 1 slice GF sourdough
+ 1 banana or fruit
~510 kcal · 45g P · 32g C · 18g F
Mel
3 frittata cups
+ 15 g goat's cheese crumbled in cups
+ Side salad leaves
~310 kcal · 30g P · 8g C · 17g F
Method
  1. Preheat oven to 180°C. Spray a 12-hole muffin tin generously with olive oil.
  2. Sauté diced onion and pepper in a pan for 3–4 min. Add turkey mince and cook through (5 min). Wilt in spinach. Season well with salt, pepper, paprika, oregano.
  3. Divide the filling evenly among the 12 muffin holes. For Mel's 3 cups, add a pinch of goat's cheese to each.
  4. Crack 1 egg into each cup (or beat eggs first and pour — both work). Top with a little extra seasoning.
  5. Bake 15–18 min until set and lightly golden. Cool slightly before removing. Store in fridge up to 4 days or freeze.
Poached eggs on sweet potato hash
25 minEgg-heavyGF/DF
Base ingredients
Sweet potato
Eggs
Red onion
Spinach

Smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt
Dave
300 g sweet potato
3 eggs poached
½ red onion
Handful spinach
1½ tsp olive oil
~540 kcal · 32g P · 62g C · 19g F
Mel
200 g sweet potato
2 eggs poached
½ red onion
Handful spinach
1 tsp olive oil
~370 kcal · 21g P · 43g C · 13g F
Method
  1. Grate or finely dice sweet potato. Dice red onion finely.
  2. Heat oil in a non-stick pan over medium-high. Add sweet potato and onion, press into an even layer. Season with paprika, garlic powder, salt. Cook 5 min without stirring, then flip and cook another 4–5 min until golden and cooked through.
  3. Wilt spinach on top of the hash for the last minute of cooking.
  4. Meanwhile, bring a pan of water to a gentle simmer. Add a splash of white vinegar. Crack eggs into a cup, swirl the water, slide eggs in. Poach 3 min for runny yolks.
  5. Serve poached eggs directly on the hash.

Method
  1. Heat olive oil in an oven-proof frying pan or cast iron skillet. Fry chorizo 2 min until it releases its oil.
  2. Add diced onion and pepper. Cook 5–6 min until softened. Add garlic, paprika, cumin — stir 1 min.

Lemon herb chicken thighs with roasted veg & quinoa
Batch-friendlyGF/DF
Base ingredients
Chicken thighs (bone-in, skin-on optional)
Courgette
Red pepper
Cherry tomatoes
Red onion
Quinoa
Lemon, garlic, olive oil, dried rosemary, thyme
Dave
2 large chicken thighs (250 g)
90 g quinoa (dry weight)
Generous roasted veg portion
1½ tsp olive oil on veg
~680 kcal · 58g P · 52g C · 22g F
Mel
1 large chicken thigh (150 g)
60 g quinoa (dry weight)
Generous roasted veg portion
1 tsp olive oil on veg
~430 kcal · 38g P · 35g C · 14g F
Method
  1. Preheat oven to 200°C. Marinate chicken thighs in lemon juice, crushed garlic, olive oil, rosemary and thyme for at least 20 min (overnight is better).
  2. Place chicken on a roasting tray. Chop courgette, pepper, red onion into chunks. Arrange on a separate baking tray with cherry tomatoes. Drizzle veg with olive oil and season.
  3. Roast chicken 35–40 min until skin is golden and juices run clear. Roast veg 25–30 min alongside.
  4. Cook quinoa: rinse, then simmer 2:1 water to quinoa for 15 min until liquid is absorbed. Fluff with a fork and season.
  5. Serve chicken over quinoa with veg alongside. Squeeze fresh lemon over everything before serving.
Turkey & black bean chilli with rice
Batch-friendlyGF/DF
Base ingredients (serves 4)
500 g turkey mince
2 tins black beans
1 tin chopped tomatoes
1 tin passata (300 ml)
2 peppers, onion, garlic
Cumin, smoked paprika, chilli powder, oregano
Chicken stock (GF), olive oil
Dave
Large bowl chilli (330 g)
160 g cooked basmati rice
½ avocado on top
Fresh coriander
~720 kcal · 55g P · 78g C · 20g F
Mel
Medium bowl chilli (250 g)
80 g cooked basmati rice
¼ avocado on top
Fresh coriander
~470 kcal · 38g P · 48g C · 14g F
Method
  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot. Brown turkey mince over high heat, breaking up with a spoon, 5–6 min. Season as you go.
  2. Add diced onion, peppers and garlic. Cook 5 min until softened. Add all spices and stir 1 min until fragrant.
  3. Add chopped tomatoes, passata, stock, and drained black beans. Stir well. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer 25–30 min, stirring occasionally, until thick and rich.
  4. Check seasoning — adjust chilli heat to preference. Cool and portion for batch cooking.
  5. Serve with basmati rice, avocado, and coriander. The chilli improves overnight — ideal for batch prep.
Pork tenderloin with sweet potato mash & green beans
30 minGF/DF
Base ingredients
Pork tenderloin
Sweet potatoes
Green beans
Garlic
Goat's butter or olive oil
Rosemary, Dijon mustard, salt, pepper
Goat's milk (for mash)
Dave
200 g pork tenderloin
300 g sweet potato
150 g green beans
1 tsp olive oil + goat's butter to mash
~620 kcal · 50g P · 62g C · 16g F
Mel
150 g pork tenderloin
200 g sweet potato
150 g green beans
½ tsp olive oil + goat's milk to mash
~420 kcal · 37g P · 42g C · 12g F
Method
  1. Rub pork tenderloin with Dijon mustard, rosemary, salt, pepper, and a drizzle of olive oil.
  2. Heat an oven-proof pan over high heat. Sear pork on all sides 2–3 min total until golden. Transfer to 200°C oven. Roast 15–18 min until internal temp reaches 65–68°C. Rest 5 min before slicing.
  3. While pork cooks, boil sweet potato cubes 12–15 min until tender. Drain and mash with a splash of goat's milk and a little goat's butter or olive oil. Season generously.
  4. Steam or boil green beans 4–5 min until tender-crisp. Toss with a little olive oil and salt.
  5. Slice pork and serve over mash with beans alongside. A squeeze of lemon over the pork brightens the dish.
Chicken & vegetable Thai green curry
Batch-friendlyGF/DF
Base ingredients (serves 4)
600 g chicken breast, diced
2 tins coconut milk (full fat)
Thai green curry paste (check GF)
Courgette, mangetout, baby corn
Onion, garlic, ginger
Fish sauce (GF), lime, coconut oil
Fresh coriander, Thai basil
Dave
200 g chicken in curry
160 g cooked jasmine rice
Generous veg portion
~680 kcal · 52g P · 60g C · 24g F
Mel
150 g chicken in curry
80 g cooked jasmine rice
Extra veg to bulk out
~450 kcal · 40g P · 33g C · 18g F
Method
  1. Heat coconut oil in a large wok or deep pan. Fry curry paste 1–2 min until fragrant. Add garlic and ginger.
  2. Add diced chicken and stir-fry 4–5 min. Pour in coconut milk and bring to a gentle simmer.
  3. Add harder veg (baby corn, courgette) and simmer 8 min. Add mangetout for final 2 min.
  4. Season with fish sauce and lime juice to balance. Simmer until chicken is fully cooked through.
  5. Serve over rice with fresh coriander and Thai basil. A wedge of lime on the side. Portion and store in airtight containers for up to 3 days — flavour deepens next day.

Batch Turkey & black bean chilli
Scales seamlessly — flavor deepens over time
To make 4 days' worth (8 portions total):
1 kg turkey mince · 4 tins black beans · 2 tins chopped tomatoes · 600 ml passata · 4 peppers · 2 onions · 8 garlic cloves · Double all spices · 400 ml GF chicken stock
Cook in the largest pot you own. Use the single-batch method but double all quantities. The chilli takes the same amount of active time regardless of scale — just needs an extra 10 min simmering. Cool completely before portioning into 8 airtight containers (or 4 large ones for 2-person servings). Cook rice separately each day for best texture — it only takes 12 min.
Freezes perfectly up to 3 months. Freeze in individual portions. Defrost overnight in fridge. Fridge-life: 4 days.
Batch Frittata cups
The ultimate high-protein breakfast hack
To make 4 days' worth (24 cups total):
24 large eggs · 300 g spinach · 2 red peppers · 200 g turkey mince · 1 red onion · 60 g goat's cheese (for Mel's 12 cups) · 2 muffin tins or bake in 2 batches
Use 2 muffin tins simultaneously in the oven for efficiency. Clearly label Mel's cups (the ones with goat's cheese) with a toothpick or colour-coded foil. Bake both trays at 180°C for 17–18 min. Allow to cool fully on a rack before stacking in containers — moisture is the enemy.
Fridge: 4 days in airtight containers. Freeze: up to 1 month. Reheat from frozen: 2 min in microwave or 8 min in oven at 170°C. Do not reheat more than once.
Batch Lemon herb chicken thighs
Roast once, map across meals seamlessly
To make 4 days' worth (8 thighs):
8–10 chicken thighs · 3 courgettes · 4 peppers · 400 g cherry tomatoes · 2 red onions · 3 lemons · 8 garlic cloves · Generous olive oil · Quinoa cooked per-day
Marinate overnight (Sunday morning, cook Sunday evening). Use two large roasting trays. Vegetables can be batch-roasted too but store separately from the chicken so they don't go soggy. Keep chicken portions (Dave: 2 thighs, Mel: 1 thigh) in separate containers. Reheat chicken in oven 10 min at 180°C or briefly in microwave. Quinoa is fast enough (15 min) to cook daily.
Fridge: 3–4 days. Freeze: 2 months (freeze without quinoa). Roasted veg fridge: 3 days max — batch only if you'll use within that window.
Batch Thai green curry
Freeze half structural macros, finish clean
To make 4 days' worth (8 portions):
1.2 kg chicken breast · 4 tins coconut milk · 4–5 tbsp GF Thai green curry paste · 2 courgettes · 200 g mangetout · 200 g baby corn · 2 onions · Double garlic and ginger
Cook in a large stockpot or casserole dish. Make 2 separate pots if your largest pan can't hold double batch comfortably — curry quality drops if it's too crowded. Store rice separately. The curry reheats beautifully in a saucepan with a splash of water/coconut milk if it thickens in the fridge.
Fridge: 3 days. Freezes extremely well — up to 3 months. Freeze in meal-sized zip-lock bags laid flat. Reheat from frozen on hob over low heat, stirring, adding a splash of coconut milk.
